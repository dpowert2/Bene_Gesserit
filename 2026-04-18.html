AI Safety & Guardrails (#2 — Reopened) New Signal

Capsule Security exits stealth with $7M runtime agent trust layer

Israeli security startup Capsule Security (Tel Aviv, founded 2025) has emerged from stealth with $7M seed led by Lama Partners and Forgepoint Capital International to build a "runtime trust layer" for agentic AI. The platform enforces guardrails inside the execution path itself — the window between when an agent receives a prompt and when it acts — without requiring proxies, gateways, or SDKs. Concurrent with the launch, Capsule disclosed two zero-day vulnerabilities: ShareLeak in Microsoft Copilot Studio (CVE-2026-21520) and PipeLeak in Salesforce Agentforce, demonstrating deep embedding in the enterprise stacks regulated institutions actually deploy. Founded by ex-Unit 8200/F5 CEO and ex-Transmit Security CTO; advisory board includes Jim Routh, former CISO at multiple Fortune 500 financial enterprises. Named a finalist at RSAC 2026 accelerator out of ~1,000 startups. This is the strongest new AI Safety signal since Overmind was archived. New startup profile: capsule-security.

Compliance — KYC/KYB Reinforces (contested sub-domain)

Spektr raises $20M Series A for AI compliance agents (Copenhagen)

Copenhagen-based Spektr raised $20M led by NEA (with Northzone, Seedcamp) to deploy networks of specialised AI agents automating KYC/KYB processes for Tier 1 financial institutions, with Santander Leasing as a named client. The founders previously built HelloFlow and sold it to Trulioo for $50M+ in under two years. The sub-domain (KYC/AML automation) has been explicitly penalised in the scoring model — bretton-ai (3★) and vivox-ai (2★) established the precedent — though Spektr's agentic architecture and high-calibre team are notable. The round reinforces that institutional compliance automation is extremely well-funded without yet providing the external agent governance layer the thesis calls for. New startup profile: spektr.

Specialist Agent — Personal Financial / S-Front Challenges (incumbent action)

Public.com becomes "first Agentic Brokerage" with AI portfolio agents

Public, the US retail investment platform, began rolling out Agents on March 31, 2026, letting investors define intent-based portfolio strategies that AI agents carry out autonomously. Rather than clicking to place trades, users specify conditions, triggers, and strategies, and agents execute continuously. Public is explicitly calling itself the "world's first Agentic Brokerage." This is a meaningful competitive intelligence signal: incumbents are building the front-end specialist agent experience themselves, compressing the window for third-party specialist agents to establish distribution. The thesis may need to focus even more sharply on infrastructure layers (safety, audit, identity, payment rails) and on specialist domain intelligence that brokerages will still need to buy rather than build (research, options structuring, tax optimisation).

Specialist Agent — Commercial Lending Reinforces (incumbent validation)

nCino unveils AI credit analyst agent for commercial lending

nCino launched its "Analyst Digital Partner," a role-based AI agent for credit professionals managing relationship reviews and portfolio risk, built on 14 years of banking-specific lending data. Early institutions report 60–70% effort reduction on credit analysis tasks. Not a startup signal — nCino is a NASDAQ-listed company — but a strong reinforcing signal that specialist lending AI agents are delivering measurable results in production at financial institutions. This validates that the commercial credit analysis category (adjacent to S2 Portfolio Construction) is moving from pilot to production at scale. No startup has yet captured this as an independent agent marketplace offering.

Agent Payment Rails (#11b) Reinforces

Lobster.cash integrates Mastercard Agent Pay for AI agent purchases

Lobster.cash, an AI agent payment solution built by Crossmint (already in the pipeline), announced integration with Mastercard Agent Pay and Verifiable Intent. AI agents can now make secure purchases using consumers' existing Mastercard cards, with Mastercard Agent Pay already live at Santander, Commonwealth Bank of Australia, DBS, and UOB. This is the Crossmint product story rather than a new company — reinforces that agentic payment rails are accelerating into production at major global banks. Nava's escrow model and Crossmint/Lobster.cash's card-based model represent two distinct architectures for the same payment authorisation problem; the card rails approach is now live in production at Tier 1 institutions.

Agent Orchestration (#1) Reinforces (likely Hard Filter candidate)

Primitive launches AI agent OS for regulated financial institutions

Derek White (ex-BBVA Global Head of Client Solutions) launched Primitive, an AI agent operating system purpose-built for regulated financial institutions, with seed funding from Fin Capital and Pelion Venture Partners. The platform offers pre-built agent templates across commercial lending, risk and compliance, and regulatory oversight, helping banks move "from fragmented pilots to governed production-scale deployment." The product appears institution-native: banks deploy Primitive to run their own agents, not to participate in a third-party agent marketplace. The Hard Filter precedent (obin-ai 1★, sycamore 1★) applies — the decisive test is whether Primitive governs external/third-party agents or the institution's own. Based on current positioning, this appears to be the latter. Notable as a competitive signal: experienced bank executives are moving into agentic infrastructure, suggesting the category is maturing.

Specialist Agent — Consumer Lending (S-adjacent) New Signal (boundary-ambiguous)

Veritus raises $10.1M to deploy voice AI agents for consumer lenders

YC S25 startup Veritus ($10.1M seed, Crosslink + Threshold + Emergence Capital) builds voice-first AI agents that handle regulated borrower conversations across phone, text, email, and chat for consumer lenders. Agents are live with fintechs, a major servicer, and a UK bank, handling application-funnel conversion and early-stage delinquency management. The boundary question: Veritus agents are customer-facing (external borrowers) but deployed exclusively by and for institutions to serve the institution's commercial objectives (conversion, debt collection). This maps closer to institutional workflow than to a third-party specialist agent in an open marketplace — but the regulated, voice-first, external-borrower-interaction angle is novel.

2026-04-15 System Human-initiated
Phase 0 coverage audit added to bg-full-cycle pipeline

New Phase 0 runs at the start of every cycle before any other work. It compares the thesis gap map against the actual pipeline, counting companies per category. Any category with fewer than 3 companies is flagged as "structurally underrepresented" and automatically elevated to critical scan priority. Any category with 0 companies is flagged as a "blind spot" and receives mandatory search allocation.

This prevents the system from endlessly deepening coverage in categories it already knows about while ignoring categories it has never searched — the structural failure that caused the platform infrastructure exposure bias.

Evidence
The v3.0 rebalance revealed that 9 of 11 specialist agent types from the thesis had zero pipeline companies, zero search terms, and zero scoring signals. Exposure normalisation and thesis anchoring catch scoring and preference drift, but neither catches the upstream problem: categories that were never searched in the first place. Phase 0 closes this gap.
2026-04-15 Thesis Human-initiated
Thesis v3.0 — Scouting rebalanced from platform infrastructure to specialist agents

Major strategic rebalance. The platform infrastructure side (Agent Auth, AI Safety, Orchestration, Compliance) is largely addressed — strong candidates and POC partners exist. The system's exposure bias flooded the pipeline with security/auth companies, creating a false signal that these were the highest priority. In reality, the thesis describes 14 stages of how elite traders work and maps 11 specialist agent types — but only 2 had any pipeline coverage.

Nine specialist agent categories added to primary scouting: Research & Signal Intelligence, Portfolio Construction & Risk, Trade Structuring, Options & Derivatives Strategy, Execution Quality, Position Monitoring, Financial Planning, Behavioural Coaching, Post-Trade Analysis. These agents are what makes the app store worth using.

Evidence
Dave identified that the high upvote rate for Auth (6/6, 100%) and Safety (5/5, 100%) reflected availability bias — those categories dominated the pipeline because search terms favoured them, not because they were the most important remaining gaps. Of 12 search categories, 10 were platform infrastructure. Of 11 thesis specialist agent types, 9 had zero search terms and zero pipeline coverage.
2026-04-15 Search Terms Human-initiated
Search terms v3 — 9 new specialist agent categories, ~95 new queries

Added categories S1–S9 covering the full investment process: research intelligence, portfolio construction, trade structuring, options strategy, execution quality, position monitoring, financial planning, behavioural coaching, and post-trade analysis. Total queries expanded from ~155 to ~250.

Platform infrastructure categories downgraded: Auth (🔴→🟠), Safety (🟡→🟠), Orchestration (🟠→⚪). Research & Signal Intelligence and Portfolio Construction elevated to critical priority.

Evidence
9 of 11 specialist agent types from the thesis had zero search terms. The pipeline had zero companies in research intelligence, portfolio construction, trade structuring, options strategy, execution, position monitoring, financial planning, behavioural coaching, or post-trade analysis.
2026-04-15 Preferences Human-initiated
Scoring bonuses added for specialist agents, infrastructure bonuses capped

New scoring bonuses: Research & Signal Intelligence (+18), Portfolio Construction & Risk (+18), Trade Structuring (+15), Options & Derivatives (+15), Execution Quality (+15), Position Monitoring (+12), Financial Planning (+12), Behavioural Coaching (+8), Post-Trade Analysis (+8). Platform infrastructure bonuses (Auth +15, Safety +12) marked as CAPPED with exposure bias warning.

Evidence
Existing preference bonuses were derived from a pipeline skewed 10:2 toward platform infrastructure. The high upvote rates for Auth and Safety reflected pipeline composition, not strategic priority. Specialist agent bonuses set based on thesis stage mapping and strategic importance to the app store customer experience.

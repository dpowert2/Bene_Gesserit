Bene Gesserit — Apr 17, 2026 Europe Deep Scan · Caroline Federal

Second run of the day, targeted: Europe-only, never-carded-before, focused on Caroline Federal's "Investable Everything" vectors (product inversion + tokenisation / fractionalisation). 49 candidates surveyed, 12 score above the bar. Headline finding: Europe is disproportionately strong on regulated tokenisation infrastructure (eWpG, DLT Act, DLT Pilot Regime) and disproportionately weak on retail-facing product inversion. The most complete full-stack picture on the continent is the Cashlink → Tangany → 21X triple in Germany.

Competitive Intelligence

Tokenization — Infrastructure Regulatory unlock

Germany's eWpG → DLT Pilot stack is live — Cashlink + Tangany + 21X now compose end-to-end tokenised securities rails

For the first time in Europe there is a complete stack: Cashlink as BaFin crypto-securities registrar, Tangany as BaFin custodian holding €3B+ in digital assets, 21X as the first EU-licensed DLT Trading & Settlement System under the DLT Pilot Regime. All three are cards in today's run. Any European issuer that wants to tokenise a bond or fund under eWpG now has a default regulated plumbing — which is exactly the infrastructure-layer convergence BG has been watching for.

Tokenization — MMF Product first

Spiko has crossed ~$1B AUM in tokenised euro and dollar money-market funds — Europe's answer to Ondo / BUIDL

Paris-based Spiko is the only AMF-approved tokenised MMF issuer in the EU. 24/7 on-chain redemption, integrations with Amundi and CACEIS, plus SG-Forge EURCV/USDCV stablecoin plumbing. Tokenised cash is the gateway drug for every other RWA flow — having a regulated European primitive running at ~$1B is the single most important product-layer fact in this scan.

Tokenization — Funds Incumbent-grade

IZNES running ECB and Banque de France tokenised-settlement pilots, with Euroclear on the cap table

IZNES is the only European fund-tokenisation platform actually settling subscriptions on-chain with the ECB and Banque de France participating. Euroclear has taken a strategic stake. Fund tokenisation at UCITS scale is the sleeper of the Caroline Federal thesis, and IZNES is positioning as a serious candidate to become the "Euroclear for tokenised funds" under the DLT Pilot.

Product Inversion — ETF issuance Seed-stage

NaroIQ raises $6.5M seed to become the "Stripe-for-funds" European ETF issuance rail

Cologne-based, Magnetic-led, General Catalyst re-upping. Board includes operators from Trade Republic, N26, Liqid, Plaid. The thesis is cleaner than it looks at first glance: inverting fund issuance so any bank or fintech can launch its own ETF is a bigger prize than a single consumer app, and would become the default European rail for every thematic / factor / private-market basket over the next cycle. Seed stage keeps it inside the BG shaping window.

Berlin, with tier-1 distribution partners Natixis and Muzinich already live. Product Inversion — Prediction markets Regulatory first

Matchbook Predictions: first FCA-authorised UK prediction market, launching event contracts Q1 2026

Event contracts are a clean Caroline Federal inversion — turning a derivative that used to be institution-only into a retail UX. Being first-and-licensed in the UK matters because European regulatory mood on prediction markets is hardening (Netherlands / France / Spain already restrictive). Whoever is live while the rules are being written has outsized optionality.

Caroline Federal — Gap worth watching Commissioned build?

No credible European retail options / structured-product UX startup exists outside the incumbent neobrokers

The long tail of the inversion scan is almost entirely neobroker feature-set (Trade Republic, Scalable, Revolut) or gambling-adjacent (Smarkets, Predict Street). A purpose-built FCA- or MiFID II-licensed retail options venue doesn't yet exist on the continent. Worth flagging to Caroline Federal as a commissioned-build opportunity rather than a pipeline find.

### 4. FINRA's 2026 Oversight Report flags agentic AI as a novel supervisory risk category
**Gap:** Regulation | **Type:** Reinforces
**Source:** [Fintech.Global — Three compliance priorities for 2026](https://fintech.global/2026/04/07/three-compliance-priorities-every-financial-firm-needs-in-2026/)

FINRA's 2026 Regulatory Oversight Report formally identifies AI agents — systems that act autonomously without predefined rules — as a distinct risk category requiring novel oversight frameworks. Key concerns include agents acting beyond their intended scope and authority, auditability gaps in multi-step reasoning chains, and the absence of guidance from the SEC on how longstanding record-keeping obligations apply to agentic outputs. FINRA expects firms to log prompts, capture outputs, track tool usage, and restrict agent system access. For you, this regulatory gap is your moat: every firm deploying agents in regulated workflows needs exactly this infrastructure — and regulators are telling them they must have it.

S-Front (Regulated Retail Front-Door) New Signal

Era Registers as SEC RIA — Fiduciary AI Agents Go Live

Era (San Francisco, ex-Stripe founders) has registered with the SEC as a Registered Investment Adviser with no human advisors — the firm's AI agents (routing through Claude, GPT, and Gemini) carry fiduciary authority to move money, invest funds, and rebalance portfolios autonomously. With 15,000 consumer app users and $9.1M in seed funding, this is the clearest current embodiment of the "Regulated Retail Front-Door" sub-track: a consumer-facing AI platform with SEC registration and full fiduciary responsibility delegated to third-party AI systems. New startup profile created: era.

Adjacent / Autonomous Specialist Agent New Signal

Panta (YC W26) — AI Agents Run a Licensed Insurance Brokerage

Panta (YC W26) is a licensed commercial insurance brokerage where the entire ops layer — submissions, carrier follow-ups, COIs, endorsements — is handled by autonomous AI agents with no human brokers. Clients include SpaceX suppliers and Marvel Entertainment, operating in the hard-to-place Excess & Surplus market ($125B annual premium). In production since December 2025. The thesis fit is indirect: Panta proves that AI agents can operate as licensed financial service providers in regulated markets, which is structural validation of the app-store model. Panta itself is vertically integrated rather than marketplace-facing, so direct score is low — but watch for an API/developer layer.

Autonomous Specialist Agent Reinforces

Harper Raises $47M — AI Insurance Brokerage Scales

Harper (YC W25, Emergence Capital Series A), an AI-native insurance brokerage for SMBs, has raised $46.8M — the same model as Panta but 12 months ahead and at scale. Harper handles 1,000+ customers/month versus 20–30 for a human-led brokerage, with 5,000 customers and 160+ carrier relationships. This is notable because it validates the AI-native brokerage model at commercial scale, providing a benchmark for Panta's trajectory. Neither Harper nor Panta is a direct BG thesis match, but together they reinforce that AI agents are becoming the operating layer for regulated financial services firms.

Competitive Intelligence / S-Front Demand Signal Reinforces

Public.com Becomes First "Agentic Brokerage"

Public.com has rolled out investor-facing AI agents that monitor markets and execute trades against user-defined portfolio strategies — marketing itself as the first "Agentic Brokerage." This is a competitive intelligence signal: if retail brokerages are building their own agentic portfolio management, the window for independent specialist agents (S2 Portfolio Construction, S3 Trade Structuring, S6 Execution) potentially narrows unless those agents can work across multiple brokerages rather than inside a single platform. It also validates the consumer demand signal for autonomous portfolio management.

Regulatory Environment / Pre-Trade Compliance & AI Safety Reinforces

FCA Formally Assesses Agentic AI Risks and Competition Shifts

The FCA has formally announced it is assessing risks and competition implications of agentic AI in financial services, with SM&CR accountability confirmed as applicable to AI-driven decisions. This regulatory signal reinforces two thesis gaps simultaneously: (a) the Pre-Trade Compliance gap deepens as regulators clarify that institutions remain responsible for AI agent decisions, and (b) the AI Safety & Guardrails gap gains regulatory urgency as the FCA scrutinises agent governance. UK-native founders building in these gaps now have a direct regulatory tailwind.

S-Front / Agent Platform Reinforces

Fincite Outlines 4 AI Use Cases for Advisers — Platform Signal

German wealthtech Fincite (backed by Deutsche Börse) has published its 4 active AI agent use cases for financial advisers: automated client profiling, portfolio rebalancing recommendations, compliance document generation, and meeting preparation. Relevant as a signal that European incumbents are now productising the exact specialist agent categories (S2 Portfolio Construction, S8 Financial Planning) that BG is tracking as open gaps — though Fincite serves internal adviser workflows rather than third-party agents. The European angle and the public articulation of specific use cases is useful competitive intelligence for the thesis.

R1 — Retail Derivatives / Prediction Markets New Signal

Elastics — AI Agents for Prediction Market Trading (Warsaw, €1.7M Pre-Seed)

Warsaw-based Elastics closed a $2M pre-seed led by Paris VC Frst, with angel participation from ElevenLabs, XBTO, RedStone, and a16z-affiliated individuals. Founded by Szymon Pawica (ex-Goldman Sachs quant analyst) and Mateusz Brodowicz (mathematician, previously built quant models for a prediction markets fund), the company gives individual traders access to institutional-grade quant strategies via plain-language AI agents that execute autonomously across Kalshi (regulated), Polymarket, and Limitless. The regulated Kalshi integration is the most finserv-credible entry point and the cleanest regulatory anchor for a potential conversation. Main concerns: the company self-describes as an "AI-native operating system for prediction markets" — the OS framing triggers the novelty gate; score adjusted accordingly. Substantial crypto exposure through Polymarket also reduces pure-finserv fit. New startup profile created: elastics.

S9 — Behavioural Coaching Agent New Signal

The Balanced Investor Club — First S9 Pipeline Entry: AI Behavioural Finance Companion (US)

Founded February 2026, The Balanced Investor Club launched its AI-native behavioural finance companion commercially — the first pipeline entry for the S9 (Behavioural Coaching Agent) gap, which has had zero companies until this cycle. The product helps everyday investors understand their own decision-making patterns, anchored on Morningstar's "Mind the Gap" finding that poor investor timing costs ~1.2 percentage points per year (roughly 15% of aggregate returns over a decade). Covers 13,000+ US stocks, ETFs, and crypto instruments; FX planned for late 2026. Explicitly educational only — no investment advice, no execution. The gap: no agent-callable API layer currently. For the thesis to fully apply, this product would need to be exposed as a callable service that other agents can invoke when a user's behaviour signals panic-selling risk. Track for potential B2B or API pivot. New startup profile created: balanced-investor-club.

Sponge (YC W26) — Financial Infrastructure for the Agent Economy (already in pipeline, Apr 29)

YC Winter 2026. Three founders are all ex-Stripe Crypto: Eric Zhang built Stripe's stablecoin payments; Jae Choi worked Stripe Ledger/Balances; Rishab Luthra built Stablecoin Financial Accounts. The regulated financial services deployment path runs through the bank/card layer, not the crypto layer. Note: Sponge was added to the pipeline on Apr 29 — this is a reinforcing surface of a company already tracked.

S-Front — Personal Financial Agent Challenges ⚠️

OpenAI Acquires Hiro Finance — Personal Finance AI Going Big Tech

OpenAI acquired Hiro Finance (Ethan Bloch, ex-Digit; backed by Ribbit, General Catalyst, Restive) in April 2026. The product — AI-native financial scenario planning for consumers — stopped functioning April 20; data deletion May 13. This is the second PFM-adjacent acqui-hire for OpenAI in six months (preceded by Sujith Vishwajith from personal finance app Roi, October 2025). Signal: OpenAI is building a "Personal CFO" into ChatGPT for Plus and Enterprise by Q3 2026. This validates the S-Front thesis but raises a distribution headwind: if OpenAI owns the consumer front door, independent personal financial agents face structural compression. The opportunity remains in the specialist layers behind the front door (S1–S9) that even OpenAI won't build vertically.

R1 — Retail Derivatives / Prediction Markets Reinforces

Prediction Markets Becoming Algorithmic Trading Venues as AI Moves In

Finance Magnates reports that as AI agents flood into prediction markets (Kalshi, Polymarket), these venues are evolving from human-driven opinion markets toward algorithmic trading venues with tighter spreads and faster price discovery. Individual traders without automation are increasingly at a disadvantage — exactly the structural gap Elastics is targeting. This is a second independent data point reinforcing R1 (Retail Derivatives) and the democratisation angle for prediction market agents specifically. The pattern mirrors equities circa 2010: as algorithmic players dominate, the retail floor shifts from "have an opinion" to "have better tools."

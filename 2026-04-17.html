Cat 7 — Agent Payment Rails New Signal

SolvaPay — European payment rails for the agent economy (€2.4M pre-seed)

Stockholm-based SolvaPay has raised €2.4M pre-seed from Redstone, MS&AD Ventures, and Antler to build machine-native payment rails that let AI agents discover, negotiate, and pay for services autonomously across platforms. The founding team brings over 50 years of combined experience at FIS, Bank of America, Lehman Brothers, and Handelsbanken — heavy regulated financial services DNA. This directly answers the "Europe equivalent" request from your recent scan cycle (Serafis 5★ note: "Can we also find things like this in the EU?"). At pre-seed, you have maximum opportunity to shape product direction toward the regulated financial agent context.

S2 — Portfolio Construction & Risk Agent New Signal

Pillar — Autonomous hedge book agent backed by a16z ($20M seed)

Pillar has raised $20M seed led by Andreessen Horowitz to build an AI agent that autonomously constructs and manages hedge portfolios for commodity-exposed businesses — ingesting ERP data, contracts, spreadsheets, and even WhatsApp messages to continuously monitor exposure and execute trades. The market is industrial commodity businesses (metals, recycling, freight) rather than investment portfolios, which limits direct fit. However, Pillar is the clearest demonstration of the S2+S5 pattern in production: live portfolio construction, autonomous execution, and continuous risk monitoring — all without a human in the loop for routine adjustments. Worth watching as a proof-of-concept for the investment agent stack. The $20M seed is large and may place this past the shaping window.

Cat 5 + Cat 7 + Cat 10 — Multi-Gap Reinforces

Nava emerges from stealth — multi-gap financial agent guardrails ($8.3M seed)

Nava emerged from stealth with an $8.3M seed led by Polychain and Archetype (joined by FalconX, Hack VC, and Eigen Labs contributors) to pioneer programmable guardrails for autonomous financial agents. The architecture combines escrow, policy enforcement, and dispute resolution: an agent proposes a transaction, Nava verifies it against your defined policy, and posts the reasoning publicly on-chain for audit. This simultaneously hits three validated pipeline gaps — safety guardrails (Cat 5), payment rails with verification (Cat 7), and dispute resolution (Cat 10). Previously evaluated and scored 5★ in your pipeline. The public funding announcement confirms viability. Key diligence question: can the on-chain verification architecture work in TradFi without requiring blockchain infrastructure from the institution?

Cat 5 — AI Safety & Guardrails Reinforces

Geordie AI wins RSAC 2026 Innovation Sandbox

Geordie AI — already in your pipeline, backed by Ten Eleven Ventures and General Catalyst, team from Snyk, Veracode, and Darktrace — has been named the winner of the RSAC 2026 Innovation Sandbox contest, receiving an additional $5M prize investment. Geordie's platform discovers AI agents running across code, cloud, and endpoints, maps each agent's tools and connections, and provides runtime observability and risk mitigation. With Overmind now archived ("not interested in participating"), Geordie is the strongest remaining Cat 5 candidate. The RSAC win validates both the team and the agent governance problem space — this is now the highest-profile agentic AI safety company in the market.

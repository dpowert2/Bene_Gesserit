Wall Street Launches First Prediction Market ETFs — Retail Event Contracts Go Mainstream

Roundhill launches six CFTC-regulated event contract ETFs on May 5 — BLUP/REDP (presidential), BLUS/REDS (Senate), BLUH/REDH (House) — wrapped as standard brokerage-account instruments accessible in retirement accounts. Bitwise ("PredictionShares") and GraniteShares filed identical slates in February. The mechanism: swaps tied to binary event contracts that settle at $1 or $0. This is the most significant retail-democratisation step for prediction markets since Robinhood brought options to mobile — it brings the Kalshi/Polymarket mechanic into every Schwab account, which is the exact "investable everything" thesis shape.

5c(c) Capital Raises $35M to Back Prediction Market Infrastructure Startups

A dedicated VC fund named after Section 5c(c) of the Commodity Exchange Act, founded by former Kalshi operators and backed by Kalshi CEO Tarek Mansour, Polymarket CEO Shayne Coplan, Marc Andreessen (via Moneta Luna), and Ribbit Capital founder Micky Malka. Plans to back ~20 early-stage startups: market makers, prediction market index designers, data tools, liquidity provision, compliance systems. First close expected within weeks of the March 23 announcement. The combination of the two leading exchange operators backing the same fund — despite active rivalry — signals strong consensus that prediction market infrastructure is the next category to compound. This is a direct pipeline seeder for the Versus/Noise/Kalshi archetype the thesis tracks.

Gap #11 — Dispute Resolution & Agent Liability New Signal (new startup detected)

Testudo Expands AI Liability Capacity to $9.25M — Lloyd's Lab Insurer Enters the Agent Risk Gap

Testudo (London, Lloyd's Lab cohort) is building specialised insurance for GenAI liability risks — covering legal costs and damages from AI-generated outputs where standard commercial general liability (CGL) now explicitly excludes. Berkshire Hathaway, Chubb, and Travelers all received state regulatory approval to carve GenAI out of their CGL policies from January 1, 2026, leaving a hard coverage gap. Testudo responds with active liability monitoring (proprietary tech scanning US AI litigation datasets in real time), policies targeted at middle-to-large enterprises, and Lloyd's market capacity partners QBE and Atrium. Investors include IA Capital Group (finserv specialist VC, lead), LocalGlobe, Goldman Sachs scouts, and Josh Buckley. This is the strongest new entry in the agent insurance subcategory since AIUC.

Gap #11 — Dispute Resolution & Agent Liability Reinforces

Big Insurance Backs Away From AI Risk — Opening Widens for Specialist Insurers

Berkshire Hathaway, Chubb, and Travelers have sought — and received — state regulatory approval to exclude AI-related damages from general liability policies. More than 80% of such requests were approved. The gap this creates is structural: enterprises deploying AI agents in financial services now have zero fallback in their standard liability stack for AI-generated harm. This structurally enlarges the addressable market for specialist AI liability insurers (Testudo, AIUC, Armilla), validates the thesis that Gap #11 is a genuine emerging market, and strengthens the rationale for both Testudo and AIUC as portfolio candidates.

S-Front / S8 — Personal Financial Agent Challenges

OpenAI Acquires Hiro Finance — Direct Competitive Entry into Personal Financial Planning

OpenAI acquired Hiro Finance (founded 2024, AI-powered personal CFO for scenario modelling and financial decisions) in April 2026, its second fintech talent acquisition in rapid succession. Hiro shut down consumer operations on April 20 and will delete all data May 13 — Bloch and team are headed to OpenAI to build financial guidance capabilities directly into ChatGPT. This is a direct challenge to the S-Front and S8 categories: if OpenAI ships an AI personal CFO natively in ChatGPT, it compresses the differentiation window for standalone personal financial agent startups. The stronger implication for your strategy: the consumer front-door layer may be won by hyperscalers faster than expected; the specialist infrastructure they need (pre-trade compliance, execution quality, portfolio construction agents) remains unaddressed and harder for OpenAI to own.

Gap #2 — AI Safety & Guardrails New Signal (new startup detected)

Portia AI Raises £4.4M — Controlled Agent Platform from Former Stripe UK CTO

London-based Portia AI (General Catalyst + First Minute Capital, £4.4M seed) builds an open-source SDK and cloud platform for AI agents that need to be predictable, controllable, and human-in-the-loop — targeting regulated industries including financial services as a primary use case. Founded by Emma Burrows (former CTO of Stripe UK) and Mounir Mouawad (former Google product lead). Three pillars: predictability (structured planning with step-by-step execution), controllability (human input when needed), and authentication (built-in secure tool access). Open-source SDK signals composability preference. The Stripe founding team gives genuine finserv credibility in the API/infrastructure layer. Positioned in the agent safety/oversight gap — closer to the controllability and audit angle than pure security. Weaker on finserv specificity than Manifold or Kai, but the team and General Catalyst backing make it worth a card.

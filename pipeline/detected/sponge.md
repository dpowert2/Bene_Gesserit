## One-liner
Financial infrastructure giving AI agents their own bank accounts, cards, and crypto wallets so they can transact autonomously without human intervention.

## FinServ Signal
Backed by Y Combinator (W26 batch). The three founders are all ex-Stripe Crypto with deep payment infrastructure experience: Eric Zhang built and launched Stripe's stablecoin payments and fiat-to-crypto onramp; Jae Choi worked on Stripe's Ledger/Balances systems; Rishab Luthra built Stripe Stablecoin Financial Accounts. This is exceptional payments infrastructure pedigree. Two products: Sponge Wallet (per-agent bank account + card + crypto) and Sponge Gateway (agent-discoverable service catalog with autonomous payment).

## Team
- **Eric Zhang (co-founder):** Staff SWE at Stripe (5.5 yrs), personally built stablecoin payments and fiat-to-crypto onramp.
- **Jae Choi (co-founder):** SWE at Stripe (6 yrs), Crypto / Ledger / Balances.
- **Rishab Luthra (co-founder):** SWE at Stripe Crypto (3.5 yrs), Stablecoin Financial Accounts.
- **Investors:** Y Combinator (W26).

## Thesis
Sponge is building exactly the financial autonomy layer the agent marketplace needs: every agent needs its own account to hold, spend, and receive funds independently. The Stripe pedigree is the strongest possible signal for payments infrastructure execution. The main friction is the crypto-heavy architecture (Base/Ethereum/Solana) — P8 has a crypto exclusion flag in the search terms. However, the bank account and card layer is straightforwardly traditional finance, and the underlying problem (agents can't transact without human intervention) is the same regardless of rail. Worth tracking as a hybrid play, with the understanding that the regulated financial services deployment path runs through the bank/card layer, not the crypto layer.

⚠️ **Crypto note:** Sponge's wallet includes EVM (Base/Ethereum) and Solana crypto. P8 carries a ⊗ crypto exclusion flag. Score adjusted down slightly; the bank account/card components remain fully thesis-aligned.

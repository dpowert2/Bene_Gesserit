Investor commentary positions Greenboard as the firm-level compliance layer that lets revenue teams move at speed — answering employee compliance questions instantly, routing decisions to the right compliance expert, and preparing compliance tasks automatically for human sign-off.

## Thesis
Greenboard is the US securities-compliance counterpart to Spektr's KYC/KYB (Denmark) — both are agentic compliance plays, but operating on different regulatory surfaces. Spektr automates external-party verification (customers, businesses). Greenboard automates internal compliance workflows (employee questions, books-and-records review, decision routing). For Bene Gesserit, Greenboard is more interesting because its surface area overlaps directly with what any agentic personal-finance product will need to navigate: SEC/FINRA-style securities compliance at the firm level. If the long-arc thesis is that personal-finance agents must operate inside regulated structures (broker-dealer, RIA), then Greenboard is the reference architecture for the compliance layer that wraps the agent. The conversational interface (GreenboardGo) is the right shape — compliance-as-conversation rather than compliance-as-forms. Caveat: KYC/AML auto-penalty doesn't apply here because Greenboard's primary surface is securities compliance (sales practices, books-and-records, supervision), not KYC/KYB. Score reflects that this is genuinely a Studio reference architecture for the regulated front-office.

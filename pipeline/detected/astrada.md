## One-liner
The data layer for autonomous finance — a single API connecting finance platforms to all three major card networks for both human and AI agent-driven spend.

## FinServ Signal
Visa and Mastercard both participated as strategic investors in a $3.8M seed — an exceptionally rare validator signal at this stage. Co-led by Bain Capital Ventures, QED Investors, and Nyca Partners (three of the most credible fintech seed/early-growth funds in the world). The company has already handled $750M+ in card spend and 3M+ transactions since launching in 2024. Customers include Workday, Zoho, Payhawk, and Miter — real enterprise fintech names, not a pilot list.

## Team
Founded by Salman Syed, an early employee at Marqeta who led the company's North America business through its Nasdaq IPO. Marqeta is the closest comparable company (card issuing infrastructure) — Syed is taking the lesson from that experience and applying it to AI-agent-native card infrastructure.

## Thesis
The core gap in your scouting thesis is the payment rails layer that lets AI agents spend money autonomously — authenticated, permissioned, real-time. Astrada positions itself as exactly that: "the data layer for autonomous finance," providing real-time data connectivity for both human and agent-driven spending across all three card networks via a single API. This is P8 incarnate — not crypto, not blockchain, but card-network-native agent payment infrastructure. The Visa + Mastercard strategic participation signals the two largest card networks are placing a bet on Astrada as a potential standard-setter for this layer. That's the same pattern FIX followed in electronic trading and what SWIFT followed in correspondent banking — the incumbents back the emerging protocol player rather than build it themselves.

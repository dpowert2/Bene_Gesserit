## One-liner
An AI operating system for financial institutions — AI agents that connect custodians, CRMs, portfolio management tools, underwriting systems and policy admin platforms to transform client onboarding and servicing.

## FinServ Signal
Investors Vanguard, SEI, Guardian Life, and Mercer Advisors all participated in the Series A — these are among the most conservative institutional capital allocators in the world. Vanguard and SEI endorsing an early-stage AI platform is an extremely unusual signal; it means they've cleared Avantos through their own due diligence and vendor risk processes. Total raised: $35M ($10M seed led by E14 Fund in Sept 2024, $25M Series A led by Bessemer in Feb 2026).

## Team
Founded by Bassam Chaptini and Rabih Ramadi. Background in financial services; team includes engineers from Salesforce and enterprise fintech. Less visible than some founder profiles but the investor calibre more than compensates.

## Thesis
Avantos sits at the orchestration layer for financial institution AI — it connects the fragmented data environments that wealth managers, insurers, and asset managers operate in (custodian + CRM + portfolio management + policy admin) and surfaces AI agents on top. This maps to P1 (Agent Orchestration) in your architecture: not specialist execution but the connective tissue that enables agents to see and act across a firm's full data estate. The Vanguard/SEI/Guardian Life participation means this is already deployed in regulated, conservative finserv environments — the hardest compliance barrier in the space. The main concern for BG is that Series A has passed the ideal entry window, and the US-focus means limited European deal access.

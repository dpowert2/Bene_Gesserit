## One-liner
Trent AI uses four specialised security agents to identify, rank, remediate, and track vulnerabilities in AI agents and their generated code — targeting enterprises deploying autonomous AI in production.

## FinServ Signal
Weak-to-moderate: No named finserv customers at launch. However, Stripe executives participated in the round as angels, and the product — securing AI-generated code and autonomous agent behaviour — has direct applicability to financial institutions deploying agentic AI for trading, operations, or compliance. Cambridge and London-based, which positions them well for UK-regulated market entry.

## Team
Founded by Eno Thereska (CEO, ex-AWS), Zhenwen Dai (CTO, ex-Amazon Research), and Neil Lawrence (Chief Scientist) — the latter is a prominent Cambridge University machine learning professor and former Amazon Chief Scientist for ML. LocalGlobe and Cambridge Innovation Capital led the $13M seed, with angels from Databricks and Stripe.

## Thesis
This gives you an early view of the AI agent security tooling category taking shape post-RSAC 2026. Trent AI's four-agent architecture (discover, rank, remediate, track) is a template worth watching — if you're operating as the regulated infrastructure layer, you'll need to certify or integrate with tools that verify the agents connecting to your platform. Trent's focus on generated-code vulnerabilities is especially relevant as financial institutions start shipping agentic workflows at scale.

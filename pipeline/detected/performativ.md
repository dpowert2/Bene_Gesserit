## One-liner
AI-native operating system for wealth managers and asset managers, consolidating portfolio management, analytics, compliance, client reporting, and multi-custodian data aggregation into a single cloud-native SaaS platform.

## FinServ Signal
Strong institutional validation: Series A led by Deutsche Börse Group with Rabo Investments (Rabobank's investment arm) participating. Founded in 2020, now serving small and mid-sized European wealth managers. AI-native from inception — platform uses embedded agents to automate manual processes across the investment lifecycle. The round signals that major financial infrastructure incumbents see AI-native wealth ops as a platform category worth owning.

## Team
CEO Albert Geisler Fox. Founded 2020, Copenhagen. Backed by FinTech Collective, EIFO (Danish sovereign fund), and now Deutsche Börse Group + Rabo Investments. Six years of product development pre-Series A is unusual — suggests deep workflow complexity and long sales cycles, but also strong customer retention.

## Thesis
Performativ occupies the infrastructure layer beneath the specialist agents — it's the "operating system" that aggregates multi-custodian data, enforces compliance, and provides the portfolio state that specialist agents (S2–S7) would need to operate. Deutsche Börse leading this round is a significant signal: incumbents are betting on AI-native wealth OS as the replacement for legacy systems, not retrofitting old ones. The Nordic origin is notable — Scandinavian wealth management is a sophisticated, regulation-conscious market and a credible proving ground for European expansion. Stage is Series A so BG's entry window has passed for direct investment, but it's worth tracking as platform infrastructure they build.

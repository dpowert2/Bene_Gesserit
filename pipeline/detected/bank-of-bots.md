# Bank of Bots

## One-liner
Bank of Bots (BOB) is the first financial infrastructure platform purpose-built for AI agents, robots, and drones as the primary account-holders — providing bank accounts, credit lines, and working capital loans to non-human economic actors.

## FinServ Signal
Issued the world's first loan to an AI agent: the agent applied autonomously, cryptographically signed the loan agreement using its own Ed25519 identity key, and received USDC into a self-custodied Gnosis Safe wallet on Base — the human operator completed KYC, but every transactional step was agent-initiated. Underwriting methodology borrows from traditional credit analysis but is applied to machine-generated on-chain economic behaviour — a direct analogue to credit scoring for non-human identities. Platform provides multi-chain wallets (BTC, ETH, SOL, Base), W3C Verifiable Credential identity passports, and a BOB Score (trust and creditworthiness rating from verified on-chain payment proofs).

## Team
Founding details not yet disclosed publicly. US-based, recently emerged from stealth.

## Thesis
This is the most structurally novel fintech company detected since Inflow. Where others are building agent orchestration or payment rails that humans use via agents, BOB is treating the agent itself as the regulated financial counterparty — with identity, credit, and capital. This directly addresses the capital constraint that makes autonomous agents economically fragile (an agent without a credit line stops when its pre-funded wallet empties). As you build the regulated infrastructure layer, BOB represents either a partner play (you are the compliance and identity rail they need to be regulated) or a thesis stress test — if BOB succeeds, the "regulated operating system" may need to be redrawn around machine-native financial identity.

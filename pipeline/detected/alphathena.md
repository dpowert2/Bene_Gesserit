## One-liner
AI-enhanced direct indexing platform for RIAs and fintech, exposing personalised portfolio construction, automated tax-loss harvesting, optimised allocations, rules-based rebalancing, and portfolio transition tools as agent-callable services. Won "Best Direct Indexing Platform" at the 2025 WealthManagement Industry Awards. Co-founder Tushad Driver named CTO of the Year at the 2025 ThinkAdvisor Luminaries Awards. February 2026 partnership with Bill Harris's Evergreen Wealth (the $100M-AUM AI-native RIA) — Evergreen leverages Alphathena's end-to-end portfolio management and direct indexing capabilities as its underlying engine.

## FinServ Signal
Founded explicitly to make direct indexing "faster, more accessible and more engaging through automation, machine learning and natural language generative AI." Targets RIAs, wealth managers, and fintech platforms — not retail end-users — which puts the company in the B-archetype lane (Independent Advisor / RIA) where the cycle-14 audience-typed surface menu has been most under-explored. Industry recognition is real and recent: 2025 WealthManagement award + 2025 ThinkAdvisor CTO recognition + Evergreen Wealth (Bill Harris) integration partnership. The company explicitly publishes thought-leadership on "agentic workflows will disrupt outsourced portfolio management" — the agent-callable framing is core, not retrofitted marketing.

## Thesis
Alphathena is the strongest live B-archetype match in the S4 cell — the agent-callable infrastructure layer for direct indexing and TLH that the v3.x preference signal explicitly asks for ("better if it exposed an agent" from Serafis 5★ and Kimpton AI 5★; portfolio-integrated tax intelligence confirmed by Alomana 5★). The Evergreen Wealth partnership is the cleanest possible validation: a seasoned RIA-tech founder (Bill Harris) with $100M AUM and a tax-focused mandate chose Alphathena as the engine, not a build. That's a buyer-side endorsement worth more than press.

The stage signal is clean — $4M seed in Dec 2024 sits squarely inside the seed-to-Series-A window. Industry recognition is a positive but not a stage red flag (compare Linx-Security's score-94/2★ on stage grounds; Alphathena is much earlier). The novelty gate read is also clean: the framing is "agent-callable direct-indexing infrastructure for RIAs" not "operating system for advisors" — distinct from the Marloo/Avantos/Asseta-AI 1★ shape. Geography (US-only) is a soft tiebreaker but not disqualifying.

The risk is the same one Range carries: the company sells to RIAs (the buyer) rather than building infrastructure for an external-agent marketplace. The third-party-qualifier test reads as "neutral-positive" — the platform serves third-party RIAs through APIs but the agents are the RIAs' own, not arbitrary external agents. This makes it a fit for the Greens / Studio "Intelligent Tax Agent" project specifically; less of a fit for the broader external-agent-marketplace platform thesis.

## One-liner
"AI economic control system" for engineering and finance leaders — real-time visibility into the cost, performance, and ROI of AI operations. The first platform to produce a true profit-and-loss statement for every AI agent in production. March 2026 launch of Tool Registry brings agent-initiated spend into a unified ledger; the AI Outcomes module ties cost to revenue per agent.

## FinServ Signal
Direct fit for P7 Agent Monetisation — the gap currently anchored only by Paid AI as a "pattern signal." Revenium goes further: not just billing for AI services but the full economic-control plane for agentic AI deployments. The "P&L per agent" framing is exactly what an agent marketplace needs to operationalise revenue share with third-party agent providers. No explicit regulated-finserv customers in public press, but the engineering+finance buyer profile is meaningful for the agent-economy infrastructure thesis.

## Thesis
Revenium occupies a thesis-aligned slot that's been thinly staffed: the economic plane underneath the agent marketplace. If the BG thesis (app store for financial agents) needs a way for institutions to pay third-party agents based on outcomes, Revenium is the closest live infrastructure for that — agent-initiated spend tracking, P&L per agent, ROI attribution. Stage signal is at the upper edge of the gate ($25.4M total) but the most recent round is a Seed; not yet Series A. Watch for whether the customer base develops finserv-specific deployments.

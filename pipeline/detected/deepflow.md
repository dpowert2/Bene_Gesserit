## Thesis
DeepFlow occupies the architectural slot that the BG thesis names as the right shape for compliance-side agentic AI: cross-silo orchestration where agents do the legwork (alert triage, evidence stitching, narrative construction) and humans hold the decision point. This is the same pattern Gradient Labs codified for customer ops — procedure-following, auditable, deterministic — but applied to financial-crime operations, where the regulatory bar is even higher and the cost of false negatives (missed laundering) is asymmetric. The pipeline already holds Bretton AI (Series B, $75M, San Francisco) as the senior US financial-crime agentic incumbent and Fenrock AI (YC W26, $4M, San Francisco) as the back-office bank-overlay play. DeepFlow is the UK/EU-regulated counterpart, and the cross-silo orchestration framing is more architecturally interesting than the single-agent investigator pattern. Watch-items: (1) whether DeepFlow exposes an API that other agentic-fintech vendors can compose into their own workflows, or stays an internal-bank workflow product; (2) whether NatWest's programme converts into a paid pilot or remains structured collaboration; (3) funding-round disclosure post-programme (target window: Q3/Q4 2026).

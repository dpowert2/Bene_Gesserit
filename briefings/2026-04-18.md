### 1. Capsule Security exits stealth with $7M runtime agent trust layer
**Gap:** AI Safety & Guardrails (#2 — REOPENED) | **Type:** New Signal
**Source:** [SiliconANGLE — Capsule Security launches $7M](https://siliconangle.com/2026/04/15/capsule-security-launches-7m-secure-ai-agents-runtime/)

Israeli security startup Capsule Security (Tel Aviv, founded 2025) has emerged from stealth with $7M seed led by Lama Partners and Forgepoint Capital International to build what it calls a "runtime trust layer" for agentic AI. The platform enforces guardrails inside the execution path itself — the window between when an agent receives a prompt and when it acts — without requiring proxies, gateways, or SDKs. Concurrent with the launch, Capsule disclosed two zero-day vulnerabilities: ShareLeak in Microsoft Copilot Studio (CVE-2026-21520) and PipeLeak in Salesforce Agentforce, demonstrating deep embedding in the enterprise stacks regulated institutions actually deploy. Founded by ex-Unit 8200/F5 CEO and ex-Transmit Security CTO; advisory board includes Jim Routh, former CISO at multiple Fortune 500 financial enterprises. Named a finalist at RSAC 2026 accelerator out of ~1,000 startups. This is the strongest new AI Safety signal since Overmind was archived. **New startup profile: capsule-security.**

### 3. Public.com becomes "first Agentic Brokerage" with AI portfolio agents
**Gap:** Specialist Agent — Personal Financial / S-Front | **Type:** Challenges (incumbent action)
**Source:** [PRNewswire — Public becomes first brokerage with AI agents](https://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/public-becomes-the-first-brokerage-to-introduce-ai-agents-for-your-portfolio-302729050.html)

Public, the US retail investment platform, began rolling out Agents on March 31, 2026, letting investors define intent-based portfolio strategies that AI agents carry out autonomously. Rather than clicking to place trades, users specify conditions, triggers, and strategies, and agents execute continuously. Public is explicitly calling itself the "world's first Agentic Brokerage." This is a meaningful competitive intelligence signal: incumbents are building the front-end specialist agent experience themselves, compressing the window for third-party specialist agents to establish distribution. The thesis may need to focus even more sharply on infrastructure layers (safety, audit, identity, payment rails) and on specialist domain intelligence that brokerages will still need to buy rather than build (research, options structuring, tax optimisation).

## Technical Artifact (not a startup profile)

**APEX Standard — Open Protocol for Agentic Trading** | **Type:** Reinforces (S6/Execution) | **Via:** L3_github_APEX-Standard
**Source:** [apexstandard.org](https://apexstandard.org/) | [GitHub](https://github.com/APEX-Standard/protocol)

The most significant technical signal of the cycle. APEX Standard is an open, MCP-based specification defining how AI agents communicate with brokers, exchanges, dealers, and other execution venues — "FIX for the agentic era." Created March 20, 2026 and actively developed (last push May 9, 2026). v0.1.0-alpha, community-governed (CC-BY 4.0 / Apache 2.0), with 19 mandatory tools across five domains (session, account, orders, market data, risk), 167 conformance checks, and reference implementations in TypeScript, Rust, Go, and Java. Profiles support: FX, CFD, Crypto, Derivatives (planned), Fixed Income (planned).

**Why this matters:** The emergence of APEX Standard is the S6 (Execution Agent) market's equivalent of FIX Protocol's 1992 launch — it signals that practitioners believe the category is real enough to warrant a shared open standard before any single vendor locks it down. This is not a fundable startup, but the startups that first build conformant implementations on top of APEX will be identifiable pipeline targets. Watch for commercial entities that emerge from or align with this protocol — the founders of APEX Standard or its first broker implementations are the likely BG-stage targets.

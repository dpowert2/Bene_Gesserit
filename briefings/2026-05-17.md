Greenboard announced $20M total funding (including a previously unannounced $15.5M Series A led by Base10 Partners) and the launch of GreenboardGo — a conversational AI-native layer on top of compliance books and records for broker-dealers, RIAs, and securities firms. As a P3 reinforcer, Greenboard is the US securities-compliance counterpart to Spektr's KYC/KYB (Denmark) — both are agentic compliance, different regulatory surfaces.

**Score:** 72

Melbourne-based Extraordinary Money (XMO) raised A$4M pre-seed led by Airtree (James Cameron) with Triple Bubble participating. Founded by former Up Bank executives, the team is building a predictive financial model that anticipates future financial decisions rather than reviewing past spending — explicitly positioned as a consumer finance product designed around generative AI rather than retrofitted onto banking primitives. AFSL and ACL applications are in progress. Cameron's investment thesis (quoted): "We've been watching for startups building consumer finance products specifically designed around generative AI rather than retrofitting existing banking products." Founder commentary on agents: "We absolutely expect AI to be making purchases and saving people money in bills in the future." This is a clean S-FRONT signal — agentic personal finance, native architecture, ex-neobank operator team.

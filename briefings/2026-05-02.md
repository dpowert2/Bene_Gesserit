### 3. Testudo expands AI liability capacity to $9.25M — Lloyd's Lab insurer enters the agent risk gap
**Gap:** Gap #11 — Dispute Resolution & Agent Liability | **Type:** New Signal (new startup detected)
**Source:** [Testudo expands AI liability capacity to $9.25m](https://fintech.global/2026/03/09/testudo-expands-ai-liability-capacity-to-9-25m/)

Testudo (London, Lloyd's Lab cohort) is building specialised insurance for GenAI liability risks — covering legal costs and damages from AI-generated outputs where standard commercial general liability (CGL) now explicitly excludes. Investors include IA Capital Group (finserv specialist VC, lead), LocalGlobe, Goldman Sachs scouts, and Josh Buckley. This is the strongest new entry in the agent insurance subcategory since AIUC.

### 4. Big insurance backs away from AI risk — opening widens for specialist insurers
**Gap:** Gap #11 — Dispute Resolution & Agent Liability | **Type:** Reinforces
**Source:** [Big Insurance Backs Away From AI Risk and Startups Rush In](https://www.pymnts.com/artificial-intelligence-2/2026/big-insurance-backs-away-from-ai-risk-and-startups-rush-in/)

Berkshire Hathaway, Chubb, and Travelers have sought — and received — state regulatory approval to exclude AI-related damages from general liability policies. More than 80% of such requests were approved. The gap this creates is structural: enterprises deploying AI agents in financial services now have zero fallback in their standard liability stack for AI-generated harm. This structurally enlarges the addressable market for specialist AI liability insurers (Testudo, AIUC, Armilla), validates the thesis that Gap #11 is a genuine emerging market, and strengthens the rationale for both Testudo and AIUC as portfolio candidates.

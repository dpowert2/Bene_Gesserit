## Key findings (improvements the new engine surfaced)

1. **Cited verification works and changes outcomes.** `/answer` returned structured round/stage/date+country with 7–8 citations per candidate at ~$0.005. Three candidates were caught as **stale signals** (rounds 8–29 months old) by the 90-day freshness gate — facts the old discovery-snippet path would not have established.
2. **The two-axis geo filter is sharply selective — and that IS the signal.** Most European family-fintech builders serve their *home* market (Sibstar→UK; Beanstalk/Mia/ETF4Kids/Clanq→EU instruments, not US 529s); most US-market builders are US-founded (Carefull, Backer, Luminary). The European-built / US-served intersection is genuinely thin — NestiFi is the standout. This validates the thesis's hardest constraint and tells us the real gap.
3. **Blind-spot seeding vs the freshness gate.** The radar's 90-day `max_signal_age_days` is tuned for ongoing scanning; for *first-of-cell* seeding it filters out the best existing companies. Applied a documented blind-spot exception (18-month window) so NestiFi could seed G6. **Recommended:** formalise an age-gate relaxation for 0-company cells in Phase 0/2b.

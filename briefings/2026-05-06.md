### 1. Elastics — AI agents for prediction market trading (Warsaw)
**Gap:** R1 (Retail Derivatives / Prediction Markets) | **Type:** New Signal
**Source:** [EU Startups — Elastics raises €1.7M to build AI agents for prediction markets](https://www.eu-startups.com/2026/05/warsaw-based-elastics-raises-e1-7-million-to-build-ai-agents-for-prediction-markets/)

Warsaw-based Elastics closed a $2M pre-seed led by Paris VC Frst, with angel participation from ElevenLabs, XBTO, RedStone, and a16z-affiliated individuals. Founded by an ex-Goldman Sachs quantitative analyst (Szymon Pawica) and a mathematician who previously built quant models for a prediction markets fund (Mateusz Brodowicz), the company is giving individual traders access to the institutional-grade quant strategies they previously couldn't build or afford. The regulated Kalshi integration is the most finserv-credible entry point. Main concern: the company describes itself as an "AI-native operating system for prediction markets" — the OS framing triggers the novelty gate; score adjusted down. Also carries meaningful crypto exposure through Polymarket.

### 2. The Balanced Investor Club — AI behavioural finance companion (US)
**Gap:** S9 (Behavioural Coaching Agent) | **Type:** New Signal
**Source:** [PRWeb — The Balanced Investor Club launches AI-native companion](https://www.prweb.com/releases/the-balanced-investor-club-launches-ai-native-companion-to-help-everyday-investors-close-their-gap-302761114.html)

Founded February 2026, The Balanced Investor Club launched its AI-native behavioural finance product commercially. The product helps everyday investors understand their own decision-making patterns and invest with greater discipline — anchored on Morningstar's "Mind the Gap" finding that poor investor timing costs ~1.2 percentage points per year (roughly 15% of aggregate returns over a decade). Covers 13,000+ US stocks, ETFs, and crypto instruments; FX planned for late 2026. Explicitly educational only — no investment advice, no execution. This is the first pipeline entry for the S9 gap, which has had zero companies until now. The limitation is the dashboard/companion architecture with no agent-callable API layer. Track for potential B2B pivot or API exposure.

### 3. Sponge (YC W26) — Financial infrastructure for the agent economy
**Gap:** P8 (Agent Payment Rails) | **Type:** New Signal
**Source:** [YC — Sponge: Financial infrastructure for the agent economy](https://www.ycombinator.com/companies/sponge)

YC Winter 2026 company building the financial autonomy layer for AI agents: per-agent bank accounts, cards, and crypto wallets so agents can hold and disburse funds without human intervention. All three founders are ex-Stripe Crypto (Eric Zhang built Stripe's stablecoin payments; Jae Choi worked Ledger/Balances; Rishab Luthra built Stablecoin Financial Accounts). Two products: Sponge Wallet (per-agent financial identity) and Sponge Gateway (agent-discoverable service catalog with autonomous payment). The Stripe pedigree is exceptional; the traditional banking layer (bank account + card) is fully thesis-aligned. Main friction: the wallet architecture includes EVM/Solana crypto rails, which P8 flags as a concern. The regulated financial services deployment path runs through the bank/card layer; worth engaging on that thesis specifically.

### 1. Adfin — Agentic B2B Money Movement Platform
**Gap:** P8 (Agent Payment Rails) | **Type:** New | **Geo:** London, UK | **Round:** $18M Series A | **Age:** 2d | **Via:** P8_layer1b_tech.eu_2026-05-14
**Source:** [Tech.eu](https://tech.eu/2026/05/12/with-18m-in-new-funding-adfin-expands-ai-powered-business-finance/) | [Next Web](https://thenextweb.com/news/dfin-18m-series-a-index-ventures-agentic-finance)
**Score:** 80

London-based Adfin raised $18M Series A (total $30M) led by Index Ventures, with Miro founder Andrey Khusid and ex-King COO Stéphane Kurgan participating. The platform uses AI agents that act as autonomous negotiators — deciding how and when to collect B2B invoices without constant human instruction. The result is a late-payment rate of 9% for customers vs 63% nationally in the UK. The "agentic money movement" framing is precise: this is P8 rails with embedded agent-native logic, where the agent is both the payment executor and the policy engine. As a template signal, the architecture maps closely to the personal finance version of the same problem — the autonomous cashflow manager as a category is now demonstrably buildable.

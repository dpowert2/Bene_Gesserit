**Gap:** AI Safety & Guardrails | **Type:** Reinforces
**Source:** [SecurityWeek](https://www.securityweek.com/surf-ai-raises-57-million-for-agentic-security-operations-platform/)

Israeli-founded, New York-based Surf AI emerged from stealth on March 17 with $57M (Accel, Cyberstarts, Boldstart) and a platform that stitches together identity, cloud, HR, and IT signals into a context graph, then deploys AI agents to prioritize and remediate risks. ### 5. ABA and BPI urge NIST to develop voluntary guidance for agentic AI at banks
**Gap:** Regulation | **Type:** Reinforces
**Source:** [ABA Banking Journal](https://bankingjournal.aba.com/2026/03/aba-bpi-urge-adoption-of-voluntary-guidance-for-agentic-ai-use/)

The American Bankers Association and Bank Policy Institute submitted recommendations to NIST calling for voluntary, technology-neutral guidance specifically for agentic AI deployment at banks — emphasising clear permission structures, approval gates, blast-radius limits, and rollback capabilities for autonomous agent actions. This is the banking industry acknowledging that existing AI governance frameworks don't adequately address agentic autonomy, and proactively shaping what standards will look like before regulators impose prescriptive rules. For you, this is the clearest possible signal that a voluntary-first governance standard for bank-deployed agents is being written now — and whoever builds the certified infrastructure layer referenced in that standard wins the regulated deployment market.

**Source:** [PR Newswire](https://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/inflow-launches-to-power-frictionless-growth-and-monetization-for-ai-agents-302633264.html)

San Francisco-based InFlow launched in December 2025 as a payments system built from the ground up for agentic commerce: agents autonomously create accounts with third-party services, execute purchases using pre-approved organisational identity and payment credentials, and operate within real-time fraud detection and policy guardrails. The platform surfaces what's arguably the most neglected gap in agentic finance — agent onboarding and credentialing at the point of action, not just at setup. As banks and fintechs deploy agents that need to transact with external services, InFlow is building the compliance-aware identity handoff layer those transactions require.

### 8. FCA preparing AI proposals for retail financial services — expected by end of March
**Gap:** Regulation | **Type:** Reinforces
**Source:** [FCA Speech](https://www.fca.org.uk/news/speeches/fca-long-term-review-ai-retail-financial-services-designing-unknown)

The FCA's Mills Review AI Live Testing second cohort — including Revolut and Monee Financial Technologies — closed its application window in early March 2026, with the FCA's formal proposals for retail financial services AI governance expected to be published by end of March. The FCA has confirmed it will not introduce AI-specific regulation; instead it will rely on its existing principles-based framework while evolving its supervisory approach. For infrastructure targeting the UK regulated market, this creates a near-term window: firms that can credibly demonstrate compliance with Principle 6 (treating customers fairly) and SYSC 3 (systems and controls) for their agentic AI deployments will have first-mover advantage before the proposals set expectations.

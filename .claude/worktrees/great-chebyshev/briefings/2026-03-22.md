### 6. Mastercard Opens "Verifiable Intent" Standard for AI Agent Transactions
**Gap:** Agent Authentication & Security | **Type:** Reinforces
**Source:** [PYMNTS](https://www.pymnts.com/mastercard/2026/mastercard-unveils-open-standard-to-verify-ai-agent-transactions/)

Mastercard released Verifiable Intent as an open-source framework on March 5, creating cryptographic audit trails that link a consumer's identity, instructions, and transaction outcome into a tamper-resistant record. It uses Selective Disclosure (minimum necessary data sharing) and is designed to interoperate with Google's AP2 and Universal Commerce Protocol, with Google, Fiserv, IBM, and Checkout.com already committed. This is the payments rails layer answering regulators' demand for explainable, auditable agentic transactions — the emerging standard you'd need to comply with or integrate against in any regulated infrastructure play.

### 7. EU AI Act August 2026 Deadline Puts High-Risk Financial AI Systems on the Clock
**Gap:** Regulation | **Type:** Reinforces
**Source:** [Matproof EU AI Act Compliance Guide](https://matproof.com/blog/eu-ai-act-compliance-financial-services)

By August 2, 2026, all high-risk AI systems deployed in EU financial services — including credit scoring, loan approval, fraud detection, AML risk profiling, and automated decision-making affecting access to financial services — must meet the full EU AI Act obligations: risk management, human oversight, transparency, auditability, and ongoing monitoring. The EBA is coordinating a common supervisory approach across national competent authorities. With less than five months until the deadline, institutions without compliant AI governance infrastructure face material regulatory exposure, creating immediate commercial urgency for the compliance and audit capabilities at the core of the regulated infrastructure thesis.

**Gap:** Regulation | **Type:** Reinforces

The FCA has confirmed that responsibility for AI-driven outcomes sits squarely inside the existing SM&CR accountability regime — meaning CTOs, CROs, and senior managers with technology or risk oversight are personally accountable if AI systems produce harmful outcomes. ### 6. UK SM&CR: Senior Managers Cannot Delegate AI Liability to Algorithms
**Gap:** Regulation | **Type:** Reinforces
**Source:** [Can senior managers be liable under the UK regulatory regime for decisions made by AI?](https://www.addleshawgoddard.com/en/insights/insights-briefings/2026/global-investigations/senior-managers-liable-under-uk-regulatory-regime-decisions-made-ai/)

Addleshaw Goddard's 2026 analysis confirms that the UK Senior Managers and Certification Regime (SM&CR) applies fully to AI-generated decisions — firms that deploy AI agents in regulated activities must designate a senior manager who is personally accountable for the outcomes, and that accountability cannot be discharged by pointing to a model. The practical implications are stark: any bank, insurer, or asset manager deploying autonomous AI agents without a clear human-in-the-loop audit trail is creating personal liability for its CTO or CRO. This is one of the clearest regulatory signals to date that *regulated infrastructure* — not raw AI capability — is the durable competitive moat in agentic financial services.

### 7. Research: Agentic AI for Financial Crime Compliance — Architecture and Deployment

A peer-reviewed paper documents the design and live deployment of an agentic AI system for financial crime compliance at a digitally native fintech, developed through an Action Design Research process with a fintech firm and regulatory stakeholders.

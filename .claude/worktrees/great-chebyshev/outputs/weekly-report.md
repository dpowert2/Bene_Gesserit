# Agentic AI in Financial Services — Weekly Intelligence Report
**Date:** 20 March 2026
**Coverage:** Week ending 20 March 2026 (second report; prior: 13 March 2026)

---

## Overview

The pipeline grew by nine companies this week to 30 total, with the most significant expansion in AI Safety & Guardrails, which added five companies raising a combined ~$313M. The agent governance space has crossed the threshold from emerging category to active infrastructure market. Simultaneously, the identity and authorisation layer for AI agents became the primary strategic battleground in financial services: Oasis Security closed the largest dedicated non-human identity raise to date ($120M Series B), Mastercard and Santander completed Europe's first live agentic payment in a regulated banking framework, Visa launched its "Agentic Ready" initiative, and Visa Crypto Labs released a CLI for AI agent payments. Regulatory signals intensified across all major jurisdictions: the US Treasury published a 230-control-objective AI Risk Management Framework for financial services, the FCA confirmed personal liability for senior managers on AI decisions under SM&CR, and SEC/FINRA sharpened enforcement posture on AI governance for broker-dealers.

## Gap Coverage

### Agent Authentication & Security — Highest Conviction

No new companies entered the pipeline, but market validation for this gap accelerated sharply. **Oasis Security** — already one of the top-scoring and highest-rated companies in the pipeline — closed a $120M Series B led by Craft Ventures with Sequoia, Accel, and Cyberstarts, bringing total funding to $195M. ARR has grown 5x year-over-year, with the majority of customers drawn from the Fortune 500. This is the largest dedicated raise in the non-human identity space to date.

The bigger structural development is that the major payment networks moved simultaneously on agent identity. **Mastercard** and Banco Santander completed Europe's first live end-to-end payment executed by an AI agent within a regulated banking framework. Mastercard unveiled **Verifiable Intent**, an open-source cryptographic framework linking consumer identity, instructions, and transaction outcomes into a tamper-resistant audit record. **Visa** launched "Agentic Ready" and released a CLI for AI agent payments through Visa Crypto Labs. Combined with Google's AP2 protocol and Coinbase's x402, four competing frameworks for authenticated agent transactions now exist.

The strategic implication is that the payment identity layer is being actively claimed by the most trusted financial brands in the world. The differentiated startup opportunity — and therefore the partnership opportunity — is the compliance and governance layer that sits on top of whichever payment standard wins: who authorised the agent, under what rules, with what regulatory reporting, and how is that chain of accountability preserved.

**t54 Labs** and **Oasis Security** remain the pipeline's strongest candidates in this gap (both rated 5★).

### AI Safety & Guardrails — Category Breakout

This gap transformed this week from a three-company cluster into the largest and most heavily funded gap in the pipeline. Five new companies entered:

**Onyx Security** launched from stealth with $40M (Conviction Partners, Cyberstarts), 70 employees, and Fortune 500 customers already live — the most production-ready new entrant. It builds a secure AI control plane that discovers agents, monitors reasoning, and approves or corrects actions in real time.

**Goodfire** reached unicorn status with a $150M Series B (B Capital, Salesforce Ventures, Lightspeed, Eric Schmidt) for its AI interpretability platform that lets enterprises understand and modify the internal mechanisms of AI models. As the EU AI Act's high-risk provisions take effect in August 2026 — covering creditworthiness models among other financial AI — interpretability is becoming a hard compliance requirement.

**JetStream Security** raised $34M (Redpoint Ventures) to build "AI Blueprints," real-time graphs of how AI models operate across environments, with audit trails and access controls. The team includes CrowdStrike's former CPO and angels from CrowdStrike, Wiz, and Okta.

**Manifold** raised $8M (Costanoa Ventures, Cherry Ventures) to build an AI Detection and Response (AIDR) platform — the first purpose-built runtime observability platform for agentic AI — monitoring every tool call, MCP connection, and system interaction. Founded by the creators of LLM Guard, the most widely adopted open-source LLM firewall.

**ArmorCode** ($81M total) expanded its agentic AI governance platform, with Phil Venables (former Goldman Sachs and Google Cloud CISO) joining the board.

A distinct "AI control plane" product category has crystallised around continuous discovery, monitoring, and governance of deployed AI agents. This is the operational enforcement layer between agent identity infrastructure and regulatory compliance. Nvidia's open-source NemoClaw release at GTC validates the market but addresses general enterprise security, not the regulated financial services requirements (audit trails, regulatory reporting, fiduciary accountability) that define the thesis's differentiation.

**Overmind** remains the top-scoring company in the pipeline (score 90, 5★ vote).

### Compliance — Broadening

Three new companies bring the compliance gap to seven companies:

**Steward** ($5M seed, Motive Partners) is an AI-first AML/KYC compliance platform managing $100B in investor assets, purpose-built for the entity types that break traditional compliance tools: fund-of-funds, family offices, and offshore trusts. It achieves same-day onboarding in 80% of cases. The angel roster — Lemonade's co-founder, Carta's former COO, Coinbase's UK CEO — signals strong fintech operator conviction.

**Complyance** ($20M Series A, Google Ventures) deploys AI agents to automate governance, risk, and data compliance workflows, with Mastercard angel involvement confirming financial services distribution intent.

**Certivo** ($6M seed) automates supply-chain compliance using a continuously running AI agent. Though supply-chain focused today, the architecture — a persistent compliance agent that monitors regulatory changes and validates documentation — is the exact pattern financial services institutions need.

The regulatory pressure on this gap intensified significantly this week. The **FCA** confirmed that SM&CR personal liability applies fully to AI-generated decisions, and legal analysis confirmed that "delegating to algorithms does not dilute liability." The **US Treasury** published the FS AI RMF with 230 control objectives. These developments convert compliance infrastructure from a cost centre purchase into a senior management liability question.

### Tax Optimisation — Gap Narrowing

**April** ($78M total, Series B led by QED Investors) launched an embedded AI tax platform for wealth management and advisory firms, providing real-time simulations for equity compensation, Roth conversions, concentrated stock positions, and estimated payments — embedded directly in advisor workflows rather than sold to consumers.

April is the first company to directly address the preference for "intelligent tax systems that uncover benefits as you update your portfolio." Its positioning as infrastructure for the advisory layer, combined with QED Investors' deep finserv portfolio, makes it the most thesis-aligned entry in this gap to date. The pipeline now has four companies; April requires a vote to confirm alignment.

### Agent Orchestration — Stable, External Pressure

No new startups entered the pipeline this week. However, external signals indicate that orchestration standards are being defined by incumbents: the Mastercard/Santander live agentic payment, Visa's competing frameworks, and continued Salesforce Agentforce momentum all point to established players claiming this layer. **Lyzr** (Accenture-backed, on-prem banking/insurance deployment) remains the strongest unvoted pipeline entry. The pipeline has five companies.

### Personal Agent — Needs Scouting

No new companies for the second consecutive week. The pipeline remains at two companies (**Mine/MoneyGPT** and **Tendi**). This is the thinnest gap and requires increased scouting priority.

## Emerging Themes

**AI Agent Control Plane** is the most significant new theme this week. Three companies (Onyx Security, JetStream Security, Manifold) raised a combined $82M to build platforms that continuously discover, monitor, and govern AI agents at runtime. This is the operational layer between agent identity infrastructure and regulatory compliance — and it is attracting serious capital at the seed stage, suggesting buyers are already committing budget.

**Incumbent payment networks claiming agentic commerce** is the highest-urgency cross-market signal. Mastercard, Visa, and Google all moved decisively this week. The window to position as the regulated infrastructure layer is compressing. The differentiated opportunity remains the compliance and governance overlay.

**AI interpretability as compliance infrastructure** emerged with Goodfire's unicorn valuation. With EU AI Act high-risk provisions covering creditworthiness models effective August 2026, financial institutions will need explainability tools as standard compliance infrastructure.

**Regulatory acceleration** was the defining background signal of the week. Five major regulatory actions landed across the US (Treasury, SEC, FINRA) and UK (FCA, SM&CR), collectively making AI agent governance an explicit supervisory requirement rather than a best-practice recommendation. This converts the thesis's "regulated infrastructure layer" from a strategic positioning choice into a market necessity.

## Research Highlights

**Towards a Science of AI Agent Reliability** (arXiv:2602.16666) proposes formal measurement frameworks for agent reliability — consistency, fault tolerance, and predictability — that will become compliance inputs as regulators demand auditable agent behaviour in financial services.

**Agentic AI for Financial Services Modeling and MRM Crews** (arXiv:2502.05439) demonstrates multi-agent crews performing model risk management, validating that the most risk-sensitive financial processes are tractable with agentic systems.

**Agentic AI for Financial Crime Compliance** (arXiv:2509.13137) documents the live deployment of a multi-agent compliance system at a regulated fintech, finding that modular agent architectures outperform monolithic AI in both accuracy and regulatory auditability — validating composable compliance infrastructure.
